This editorial explores cutting-edge genomic research that bridges ecological understanding with molecular mechanisms.

The groundbreaking genome assembly of the Helan Shan pika (Ochotona argentata) represents a significant milestone in conservation genomics. By generating a high-quality genome with 2.22 Gb and 24,374 annotated genes, researchers have created a crucial genetic blueprint for this endangered species. The study revealed fascinating demographic insights, including a population decline coinciding with mid-Pleistocene glacial periods and an increased genetic load resulting from the species' restricted geographic range. This genomic resource not only provides critical information for understanding local adaptation but also offers a molecular framework for potential conservation strategies.
