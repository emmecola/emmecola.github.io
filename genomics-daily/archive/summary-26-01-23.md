Bozzi and colleagues made a remarkable discovery by sequencing a 5,500-year-old Treponema pallidum genome from Colombia, providing unprecedented insights into the pathogen's evolutionary history. The genome represents a sister lineage to all known Treponema subspecies, suggesting a much deeper and more complex history of treponemal pathogens in the Americas than previously understood. This research not only expands our understanding of bacterial evolution but also demonstrates how ancient genomic analysis can rewrite historical narratives about infectious diseases.
