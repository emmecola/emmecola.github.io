Freel and colleagues significantly advanced our understanding of marine bacterial ecology by comprehensively analyzing the Pelagibacterales (SAR11) order. By sequencing 81 new strains from the Pacific Ocean, they established a robust classification system defining 29 discrete ecotypes as genera. This work is crucial for comprehending marine microbiological diversity, demonstrating how genomic analysis can reveal intricate evolutionary relationships and ecological adaptations in microbial populations that play critical roles in global carbon cycling.
