Zhu et al.'s work in *Science* introduces a groundbreaking method called GRAPE (geminivirus replicon-assisted in planta directed evolution), which enables rapid genetic variation screening directly in plant cells. By leveraging geminivirus rolling circle replication, researchers can now evaluate up to 100,000 genetic variants on a single leaf within just four days. The method successfully demonstrated its potential by evolving immune receptors in plants, broadening their recognition of pathogen effectors. This technique represents a significant advancement in plant biotechnology, offering unprecedented speed and scalability in genetic engineering.

Baduel et al.'s research in *Science* reveals the critical role of transposable elements (TEs) in transgenerational epigenetic inheritance in Arabidopsis thaliana. By analyzing over 700 plant strains, the researchers discovered that DNA methylation changes at TE loci can be stably inherited across generations without altering DNA sequences. These epigenetic variations often cause gene expression changes and may be subject to natural selection, providing a novel mechanism for heritable phenotypic variation. This study challenges traditional views of genetic inheritance and highlights the complexity of epigenetic information transmission.
