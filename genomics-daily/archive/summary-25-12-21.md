The study by Chowdhury et al. introduces ec3D, a computational method for reconstructing three-dimensional structures of extrachromosomal DNAs (ecDNAs) in cancer. Li et al. conducted a comprehensive genomic investigation into thermal stress responses in Mytilus coruscus, a marine mussel. Their research unveiled intricate molecular mechanisms of heat adaptation, highlighting the crucial roles of autophagy and heat shock protein 70 (HSP70) pathways. By examining transcriptomic changes across different tissues, they discovered organ-specific thermal stress responses and identified key genes that interact to regulate cellular survival during environmental temperature fluctuations, providing valuable insights into marine organism adaptation to climate change.
